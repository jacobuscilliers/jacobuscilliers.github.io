Every year, students at McCourt School's Master in International Development Policy (MIDP) work with a client to produce an applied empirical report which answers a policy-relevant question. Past clients include: ARK, iDE, IFPRI, Mercy Corps, the Tony Blair Africa Governance Initiative, Twaweza, Peloria, USAID, the Millennium Challenge Corporation (MCC), and multiple different units at the World Bank. I have advised students through this process since 2015.

I provide some examples of previous projects below (including links to the reports and a policy brief summarizing the results).

---

<details>
<summary><strong>The Drivers and Returns to Migration</strong> — Mercy Corps</summary>

Using panel data from DRC, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka, students examined the effects of exogenous shocks on migration likelihood. Crop/livestock disease had the most significant effect, increasing the likelihood of migration by 11.2 percentage points.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/5can4ojx7zfmv44/Policy%20Brief_Updated.pdf?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/8pur6o9ojklphib/Mercy%20Corps%20Capstone%20Final%20Report.pdf?dl=0)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The 2015 Gorkha Earthquake in Nepal</strong> — World Bank</summary>

Students analyzed exposure, vulnerability, and resilience patterns following the 2015 earthquake. Poorer households experienced proportionally greater asset losses and relied more on expenditure reduction strategies.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/ncv1ousa1u8vn97/%5BPPOL548%2C%20Team%20Nepal%5D%20The%202015%20earthquake%20in%20Nepal%20%28Infographic%29.pdf?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/jne2ddrwskmmb2l/%5BPPOL548%2C%20Team%20Nepal%5D%20The%202015%20earthquake%20in%20Nepal%20%28Report%29.pdf?dl=0)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Inequality of Opportunity in Ethiopia</strong> — World Bank</summary>

Students examined trends in service access inequality between 2011-2016. Rural status is by far the largest driver of inequality, compared to income or education.

[Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/ohn2qovqp85w8t1/IOP%20Ethiopia%20Final%20Chaper_2.5.19.pdf?dl=0) | [World Bank Ethiopia Poverty Assessment](http://documents.worldbank.org/curated/en/992661585805283077/Ethiopia-Poverty-Assessment-Harnessing-Continued-Growth-for-Accelerated-Poverty-Reduction)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can Sanitation Marketing Improve Latrine Usage and Reduce Incidence of Diarrhea?</strong> — iDE (Cambodia)</summary>

Students evaluated the impact of sanitation marketing in Cambodia. Latrine coverage increased by 17.8 percentage points while diarrhea decreased by 5.8 percentage points.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/yg8oq11ea6ksgqt/Infographic_iDE_McCourt.pdf?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/j3dlhhthersp4vy/FinalReport_iDE_McCourt.pdf?dl=0)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Household Resilience to Conflict in Nigeria</strong> — Mercy Corps</summary>

Using a triple-difference strategy, students examined determinants of resilience. Villages with higher social capital effectively mitigated negative conflict impacts on child malnutrition.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/vuflr5sqr9v2t2p/Infograph%20Nigeria_v2.jpeg?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/powcdmsfrb34dxo/MercyCorpsResearchPaper_FINAL.pdf?dl=0)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Ebola's Impact on Labor Markets in Sierra Leone</strong> — World Bank</summary>

Students studied employment effects in regions with varying Ebola exposure. Farming sector employment declined in heavily exposed regions, partially offset by increased non-formal self-employment.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/jnb08psm6vi6lkt/Ebola%20infographic.pdf?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/q93udc73iawbc5j/Capstone%20Final%20Report%20Team%20Ebola.pdf?dl=0)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Violence Prediction in Iraq</strong> — Mercy Corps</summary>

Students used machine learning to analyze violence predictors. Voting behavior and tribal chief connections emerged as the strongest predictive factors.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/noah14xnibz2307/MercyCorps.jpg?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/07wr08c4l0j7k3a/Mercy_Corps_Capstone_Project.pdf?dl=0)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Private School Vouchers in India</strong> — ARK</summary>

Students evaluated a randomized low-fee private school voucher program in New Delhi. The program showed a negative impact on Hindi learning but a positive impact on English outcomes.

[Policy brief](https://www.dropbox.com/s/beaf06u72prr6kb/Ark%20Infographic%20final.pdf?dl=0) | [Technical report](https://www.dropbox.com/s/oms9vm1kh4xzlh2/Ark%20Capstone%20Report.pdf?dl=0)

</details>
